Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts or 8 bone-in chicken thighs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste for spiciness)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish
  • Cooked rice or naan bread for serving.

Instructions:

Marinate the Chicken:

If you have boneless chicken breasts, cut them into bite-sized pieces. If using bone-in chicken thighs, you can leave them whole.
In a bowl, mix the chicken with olive oil, minced garlic, ginger, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper.
Ensure the chicken is evenly coated with the spices. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to marinate.
For better results, marinate for a few hours or overnight.

Sear the Chicken:

Heat a large skillet or pan over medium-high heat. Add a bit more olive oil if needed.
Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.

Saute Onions:

In the same pan, add the finely chopped onion and saut√© until it becomes soft and translucent, about 3-4 minutes.

Add Tomatoes and Spices:

Add the diced tomatoes (with their juice) to the pan and stir well to combine with the onions.
Add the chicken broth and stir again.
Return the seared chicken to the pan and bring the mixture to a simmer.

Simmer and Cook:

Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. Stir occasionally.

Finish with Yogurt:

Once the chicken is cooked, stir in the plain yogurt. This will add creaminess to the sauce. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, just until heated through.
Do not let it boil after adding the yogurt, as it may curdle.

Serve:

Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary by adding more salt, pepper, or cayenne pepper.
Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.
Serve the Halal chicken curry over cooked rice or with naan bread.
Enjoy your homemade chicken dish!
